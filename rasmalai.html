R a s m a l a i

Ingredients:

For the Rasgullas (Cottage Cheese Balls):

1 liter full-fat milk

2 tablespoons lemon juice or white vinegar

4 cups water

1 cup sugar

For the Milk Syrup:

1 liter full-fat milk

1 cup sugar

A few saffron strands

1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

Chopped nuts (pistachios, almonds) for garnish

    Preparation:

    Making Rasgullas (Cottage Cheese Balls):

    In a large pot, heat the milk. Once it reaches a boil,
    reduce the heat to low.Add lemon juice or vinegar gradually
    while stirring until the milk curdles, and the whey separates
    from the milk solids (paneer).

    Strain the mixture through a muslin cloth,
    wash the paneer under cold water to remove the lemony taste,
    and then squeeze out excess water.
    Hang the cloth for about 30 minutes to drain excess water.

    Knead the paneer until it's smooth and no longer grainy.
    Roll it into small balls and flatten slightly to make rasgullas.

    In another pot, heat water and sugar.
    Once it boils, gently drop the rasgullas and cook covered for 15 minutes.
    They will puff up and float. Let them cool.

    Preparing Milk Syrup:

    In a wide pan, bring the milk to a boil and then simmer.
    Keep stirring occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom.

    Add sugar, saffron strands, and cardamom powder. Continue to simmer until
    the milk reduces to almost half its quantity, and it thickens slightly.
    This could take around 30-40 minutes.

    Assembling Rasmalai:

    Squeeze the excess sugar syrup from the cooked rasgullas and gently
    add them to the thickened milk.

    Allow the rasgullas to soak in the milk for at least an hour.
    They will absorb the flavors and become soft and spongy..

    Garnish with chopped nuts.

    Chill and Serve:

    Once cooled, refrigerate the Rasmalai. It's best served chilled,
    allowing the flavors to meld together.
