G u l a b j a m u n

Ingredients:

1 cup powdered milk
(also known as milk powder)

1/4 cup all-purpose flour

1/4 tsp baking soda

2-3 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)

A few tablespoons of milk
(as needed to make a soft dough)

Oil or ghee for frying

For the sugar syrup:

1 1/2 cups sugar

1 1/2 cups water

3-4 cardamom pods (optional),slightly crushed

Red Chili powder:2 tablespoon

A few saffron strands (optional)

1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

    Preparation:

    Making the Sugar Syrup:

    In a saucepan, combine sugar and water.

    Bring it to a boil.

    Add cardamom pods and saffron strands if using.

    Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the syrup slightly thickens.

    Stir in the rose water, turn off the heat, and set it aside.

    Preparing the Dough:

    In a mixing bowl, combine the powdered milk, all-purpose flour,and baking soda.

    Add ghee and mix well until it resembles a crumbly texture.

    Gradually add milk and knead the mixture into a soft, smooth dough.

    Be careful not to over-knead.

    Shaping and Frying:

    Heat the oil or ghee in a pan on medium heat for frying.

    Meanwhile, shape the dough into small, smooth balls without any cracks.

    Ensure they're uniformly sized for even cooking.

    Once the oil is hot enough (but not smoking), gently slide in a few balls at a time.
    Fry on low to medium heat, continuously rolling them for even browning.

    They will expand slightly.

    Fry until they turn a rich golden brown color.

    Remove and place on a paper towel to remove excess oil.

    Soaking in Syrup:

    While the fried balls are still warm, gently place them in the warm sugar syrup.

    Allow the gulab jamuns to soak in the syrup for at least 1-2 hours before serving,
     allowing them to absorb the sweetness.

    Serve:

    Garnish with chopped nuts like pistachios or almonds if desired.

    Serve warm or at room temperature.
