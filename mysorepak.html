M y s o r e p a k

"Mysore Pak is a traditional South Indian sweet made from gram flour, ghee, and sugar. It has a crumbly,
         melt-in-your-mouth texture and a rich taste. Here's a basic recipe to make this delightful treat:"

Ingredients:

1 cup besan (gram flour)

1 1/2 cups sugar

1 1/4 cups ghee (clarified butter)

1/4 cup water

    Preparation:

    Prepare the Tray:

    Grease a tray or a plate with ghee and keep it ready.
    The size of the tray will determine the thickness of the Mysore Pak.

    Mixing the Batter:

    Sift the besan to ensure there are no lumps.

    In a heavy-bottomed pan, add water and sugar.
    Stir over medium heat until the sugar dissolves,
    forming a syrup. Bring it to a boil and let it reach
    a one-string consistency. To check, take a bit of syrup
    between your thumb and forefinger; a single thread should form and not break easily.

    Add half the ghee to the besan and mix well.

    Gradually add the hot sugar syrup to the besan-ghee mixture,
    stirring continuously to avoid lumps.

    Cooking the Mixture:

    In the same pan, heat the remaining ghee. Once itâ€™s hot, add the besan mixture.

    Cook the mixture on low to medium heat, stirring continuously to prevent it from
    sticking to the bottom.

    As you keep stirring, the mixture will begin to thicken and change color, becoming frothy.

    Setting the Mysore Pak:

    Once the mixture thickens considerably and starts leaving the sides of the pan,
    pour it onto the greased tray. Spread it out evenly using a spatula.

    Cutting and Cooling:

    While the mixture is still warm, cut it into square or diamond-shaped pieces.

    Let it cool completely. As it cools, it will solidify.

    Serve:

    Once the Mysore Pak has completely cooled and set,
    gently remove the pieces and store them in an airtight container.
