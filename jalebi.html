J a l e b i

Ingredients:

For the batter:

1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)

2 tablespoons gram flour (besan)

1 tablespoon yogurt

1 teaspoon baking powder

Water (as needed for the batter)

For the syrup:

2 cups sugar

1 1/2 cups water

A few saffron strands (optional)

1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

A few drops of lemon juice

Oil or ghee for frying

    Preparation:

    Making the batter:

    Mix all-purpose flour, gram flour, yogurt, and baking powder in a bowl.

    Gradually add water and whisk to make a smooth, lump-free, pourable batter.
    It should be thicker than pancake batter but still runny.
    Allow it to rest for at least 30 minutes.

    Preparing the syrup:

    In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil.

    Add saffron strands (if using), cardamom powder, and a few drops of
    lemon juice to prevent crystallization..

    Simmer the syrup until it reaches a slightly sticky, one-string consistency. Keep it warm.

    Frying the jalebis:

    Heat oil or ghee in a flat-bottomed pan or a wide, shallow frying pan over medium heat.
    You can also use a piping bag or a squeeze bottle to pipe the batter into the hot oil.

    Fill the batter into a squeezable container with a narrow opening or a cloth cone
    with a small hole at the tip.

    Squeeze the batter in a spiral shape into the hot oil. You can make small or larger
    spirals as per your preference.

    Fry until the jalebis are crispy and turn a golden color. They should not be brown.
    Remove from the oil and drain excess oil by placing them on a paper towel.

    Soaking in syrup:

    While the jalebis are still warm, immerse them in the warm sugar syrup for a minute or so.
    Ensure they are coated well with the syrup.

    Serve:

    Serve the jalebis warm.
    They are best when fresh but can also be stored and reheated before serving.
    Enjoy the crispy, syrupy goodness!
