D o u b l e k a m e t h a

Ingredients:

6-8 slices of white bread
(usually double roti or white bread is used)

Ghee or oil for frying

1 cup sugar

1 cup water

A few saffron strands

1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

Chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
for garnish

    Preparation:

    Prepare the Bread Slices:

    Cut the edges off the bread slices and cut them
    into halves diagonally to form triangles.

    Heat ghee or oil in a pan and fry the bread slices
    until they turn golden brown and crispy.
    Remove excess oil by placing them on a paper towel.

    Prepare the Sugar Syrup:

    In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil,
    stirring until the sugar dissolves

    Add saffron strands and cardamom powder to the syrup.
    Simmer for a few minutes until it slightly thickens.

    Soaking the Bread:

    Dip the fried bread slices into the warm sugar syrup for
    a minute or so. Ensure they are coated well with the syrup.

    Assemble and Garnish:

    Arrange the soaked bread slices in a serving dish, layering them neatly.

    Pour any remaining syrup over the top to ensure
    the bread is moist and sweet throughout.

    Garnish with chopped nuts.

    Rest and Serve:

    Let the Double ka Meetha rest for at least an hour so the flavors meld together.

    It can be served warm or cold, as per your preference.
